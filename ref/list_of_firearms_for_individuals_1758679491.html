List of firearms: for individuals

The following firearms were classified as prohibited under the Criminal Code in May 2020, December 2024 or March 2025.

Individual firearm owners can declare these firearms when the Assault-Style Firearms Compensation Program (ASFCP) is open across the country later this fall. Firearm owners, who's claims are accepted in the program, can either turn in their firearms for compensation or permanently deactivate them and be reimbursed for a flat rate of $400 - $700 per firearm. Please note that submitting a declaration will not guarantee compensation.

The program for individuals will begin later this fall. Owners will be notified when it begins.

More information on how to participate will be provided at a later date.

Participating in the program is voluntary, however individual firearm owners must dispose of or permanently deactivate their assault-style firearms by the end of the amnesty period or risk criminal liability for the illegal possession of a prohibited firearm.

Any firearms, upper receivers or parts and components that are not eligible for compensation under the ASFCP, but are turned in through the program, will be destroyed without compensation.

Lower receivers are considered fully assembled firearms for compensation purposes under the program and have been included in the list of firearms.

See also the list of upper receivers (for disposal only).
